## Purpose

Show that the HELB boom-bust pattern and the M-Pesa tracking gap are not just my own experience — they're a pattern across students, and the pattern varies in ways worth naming (urban vs. rural institution, side income or not, HELB dependency or not).

---

## Scope — what we collect vs. what we never collect

**Never collected (hard rule, no exceptions):**
- Name
- Phone number
- Email address
- Registration / student number
- Any other identifier that could de-anonymize a response

**Collected (all optional to skip, all anonymous):**
- University / institution
- HELB recipient status
- Side hustle status (yes/no, and roughly what kind — categorical, not specific)
- M-Pesa tracking habits
- Fuliza usage and fee awareness
- Willingness to use a private, local-only tool

This mirrors Bob's own design stance — no data leaving the device, no unnecessary personal info collected — so it's worth stating explicitly in the writeup that the research method matches the product's privacy claim.

---

## Survey questions 

1. Which university/institution are you at? *(free text or dropdown — used only to categorize urban vs. rural/peri-urban campus, never to identify individuals)*
2. Are you a HELB recipient? *(Yes / No)*
3. Do you have a side hustle or secondary income source (e.g. resale, freelance, tutoring, small business)? *(Yes / No — if yes, optional: what kind, in one word)*
4. Do you check where your M-Pesa money goes after it arrives? *(Yes, regularly / Sometimes / Rarely or never)*
5. After HELB or a large M-Pesa receipt, do you run out of money before the next one arrives? *(Almost always / Sometimes / Rarely — only applicable to HELB recipients)*
6. Have you ever used Fuliza? *(Yes / No)*
7. If yes to Fuliza — did you know roughly how much it cost you in fees last month? *(Yes, I know the number / No, I had no idea / I don't use Fuliza)*
8. Would you use a tool that showed your M-Pesa spending patterns privately, with no login and no data leaving your phone? *(Yes / Maybe / No)*

Anonymous, no email collection, no login required to respond.

## Draft replacement text for WRITEUP.md placeholder

*(FINAL — closed at 30 responses, July 15, due to time constraints ahead of the submission deadline.)*

I ran an anonymous, 8-question survey (no name, phone, or email collected — the same privacy stance as Bob itself) and closed it at 30 responses, due to time constraints ahead of the submission deadline. Most respondents were from Meru University of Science and Technology and neighboring institutions, with single responses from Strathmore, Kenyatta University, and the University of Nairobi. The sample skews regionally — it reflects who the survey link actually reached in a week, not a deliberate cross-section — but the pattern inside it is consistent enough to be worth reporting honestly rather than dressed up as more representative than it is.

Of the 23 respondents who receive HELB, 87% said they run out of money before the next disbursement at least "sometimes," and 43% said "almost always." Only 37% of all respondents said they check where their M-Pesa money goes regularly — most only look after the fact, or only after a bad decision. Fuliza usage was common (67%), but among those users, 85% had no idea how much they'd paid in fees the previous month. And when asked directly whether they'd use a private, local-only tool with no login and no data leaving their phone, 73% said yes outright, 93% said yes or maybe.

The numbers say what I expected them to say, but hadn't proven before building: the HELB boom-bust pattern isn't a personal quirk, self-tracking is rare, Fuliza's cost is genuinely invisible to the people paying it, and the privacy-first design isn't a nice-to-have — it's what students said they actually want.
