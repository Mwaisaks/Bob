# M-Pesa Transaction Fees — What Each Transaction Actually Costs You

## Why fees matter
Many students track spending but ignore transaction fees. If you send money 5 times instead of once, you pay 5× the fee. Wanjiku-type users (many small sends, frequent airtime top-ups) can lose KES 200–400 per month purely to fees — that is KES 2,400–4,800 per year that does nothing.

## Fee-saving strategies

### Batching sends
If you send KES 500 to mum five times, you pay KES 7 × 5 = KES 35.
If you send KES 2,500 once, you pay KES 33.
**You save KES 2 by batching — and you make one transaction instead of five.**

For larger amounts (multiple sends adding up to KES 3,500+), the savings are larger.

### Till vs cash
Paying at a till (buy goods) is free. Withdrawing cash to pay the same merchant costs you withdrawal fees + the merchant may not give change exactly. Use till payments where accepted.

### Safaricom airtime vs third-party
Top up Safaricom airtime directly from M-Pesa (free). Avoid third-party airtime apps that charge a service fee.

### Timing Fuliza repayment
Do not repay Fuliza in installments — each additional day costs more. Repay in full the moment enough money arrives.

## The fee bleed problem
Students with irregular income (many small receives + many small sends) accumulate the worst fee load. A pattern of 6 sends at KES 300 each costs KES 7 × 6 = KES 42 in fees. One send of KES 1,800 costs KES 23. The money went to the same place — but one approach cost nearly double in fees.
