# Fuliza M-Pesa — What It Is, What It Costs, and When to Stop

## What Fuliza is
Fuliza is Safaricom's overdraft service integrated with M-Pesa. When your M-Pesa balance is too low to complete a transaction, Safaricom lends you the shortfall and completes the payment. You repay automatically from your next M-Pesa deposit.

Fuliza is not a loan application — it activates silently in the background when your balance is insufficient.

## How it works
1. You try to pay KES 200 but your balance is only KES 40
2. Safaricom automatically borrows you KES 160 from Fuliza
3. The transaction completes as if you had enough money
4. A daily charge begins immediately

## The fees
- **Accessing Fuliza**: KES 1 activation fee (one-time, paid when you first enroll)
- **Daily maintenance fee**: charged every day the balance is outstanding
  - KES 0–500 borrowed: KES 2/day
  - KES 501–1,000: KES 5/day
  - KES 1,001–1,500: KES 7.50/day
  - KES 1,501–2,500: KES 10/day
  - KES 2,501–70,000: KES 20/day
- Fees are deducted automatically when money enters your M-Pesa account

## Example cost calculation
Borrow KES 500 on Monday. Repay KES 500 on Thursday (3 days later):
- Daily charge: KES 2 × 3 days = KES 6
- Total cost for that KES 500: KES 506

Borrow KES 500 on Monday. Repay in 30 days:
- Total cost: KES 2 × 30 = KES 60 extra, on a KES 500 loan
- Effective annualised rate: around 43% — higher than most bank loans

## The dependency trap
Regular Fuliza use is a sign that spending is consistently exceeding income.
Each time you repay, your balance dips again, making the next Fuliza use more likely.
This creates a cycle: borrow → repay → balance drops → borrow again.

Students who use Fuliza 5+ times a month are typically spending more than they receive.

## Alternatives to Fuliza
- **Ziidi**: Safaricom's investment product — saves small amounts from your M-Pesa into a money market fund. Any balance in Ziidi earns interest (4–8% per year). You can withdraw to M-Pesa in minutes.
- **M-Shwari**: Safaricom/NCBA savings + loan product. Savings earn 6.5% interest per year. Loans available against your saving history.
- **SACCO short-term loans**: If your university has a student SACCO, their rates are much lower than Fuliza.
- **Family top-up**: Requesting a top-up before going negative is better than Fuliza — no fees.

## Practical advice
- If you have used Fuliza more than 4 times in a month, that is a signal, not a coincidence
- Pay off the full Fuliza balance the moment money arrives — do not let it run for days
- Set a mental rule: Fuliza is for emergencies only (medical, bus home, genuinely urgent) — not for food or entertainment
- Ziidi is a better habit than Fuliza — save KES 20–50 per day and you'll never need Fuliza for small amounts
