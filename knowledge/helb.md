# HELB — Higher Education Loans Board

## What HELB is
The Higher Education Loans Board provides loans and scholarships to Kenyan students enrolled at public and private universities and TVET institutions. It is the primary source of government financial support for university students.

## Loan amounts (2024/2025 academic year)
- Needy students: KES 60,000 per year
- Average student: KES 40,000–53,000 per year
- Students from well-off backgrounds: KES 8,000–35,000 per year
- The actual amount assigned is based on a means test — family income, assets, and number of dependants
- The loan is disbursed in two instalments per academic year (beginning of each semester)

## How disbursement works
1. HELB sends the funds directly to your M-Pesa registered number
2. You receive an SMS: "You have received KshXX,XXX.XX from HELB LOANS BOARD"
3. The full semester amount arrives in one transaction
4. Disbursement typically happens 1–3 weeks after semester begins

## The boom-bust pattern
Most students receive KES 20,000–30,000 in a single day. Without a plan:
- Week 1: Food, clothes, entertainment, clearing debts — 40–60% gone
- Week 2: Normal spending with what remains
- Week 3: Slow down begins, Fuliza use starts
- Week 4 onward: Dependent on family top-ups or Fuliza to survive

This is not a discipline problem — it is a lump-sum architecture problem. The money was never structured for weekly survival.

## Repayment
- Repayment begins 1 year after completing or leaving studies
- Interest rate: 4% per year (one of the lowest available)
- Repayment period: up to 15 years
- If you default, HELB reports you to the Credit Reference Bureau (CRB), which blocks you from getting bank loans, mortgages, and some employment backgrounds checks

## CRB consequences of HELB default
- A CRB listing stays for 5 years after you clear the debt
- Banks, SACCOs, and some employers run CRB checks
- Some landlords now check CRB before renting
- You cannot clear a CRB listing instantly — it requires full repayment plus a 5-year wait for the listing to expire

## Practical advice for HELB recipients
- Divide your disbursement into weeks immediately: split into 16–17 equal parts for 4 months
- Set a weekly spending limit and M-Pesa account rule: keep only that week's amount accessible
- Do not clear all social debts in week one — stagger them
- Track your first 7 days — the pattern you set in the first week repeats every semester
