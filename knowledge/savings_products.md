# Money Market Funds — Ziidi, Chumz, M-Shwari, and the Savings Habit

## What a money market fund (MMF) is
A money market fund pools many investors' money and invests it in low-risk, short-term instruments (government T-bills, bank deposits, commercial paper). The fund earns interest and passes it to investors daily.

For students, the important properties:
- Money is accessible (withdraw to M-Pesa in minutes, not days)
- Earns interest daily — even KES 100 sitting there earns something
- Safer than a bank savings account (usually higher rates)
- Protected by CMA (Capital Markets Authority) regulation

## Ziidi — Safaricom's MMF
Ziidi is an M-Pesa-integrated money market fund operated by Safaricom in partnership with a regulated fund manager.

**Key facts:**
- Minimum investment: KES 1
- Interest rate: approximately 4–9% per year (varies with market conditions)
- Interest credited: daily
- Withdrawal to M-Pesa: within 1 minute (up to KES 70,000 per day)
- No lock-up period — your money is always accessible

**How to use it well:**
- Auto-save on every M-Pesa receipt — even KES 10
- Use it as your "Fuliza prevention buffer" — keep KES 500–1,000 in Ziidi at all times
- Treat it as a weekly spending budget safe: move only this week's money to M-Pesa, leave the rest in Ziidi

## Chumz — student-friendly savings
Chumz is a youth-targeted savings app built on top of an MMF, designed to encourage habit formation through challenges and group goals.

**Key features:**
- Round-up savings: rounds each M-Pesa transaction up and saves the difference
- Challenge savings: set a goal (e.g., KES 5,000 for textbooks) and save toward it
- Social: you can save with friends toward shared goals (chama-style)
- Interest rate: similar to other MMFs (4–8% per year)

**Best for:** students who struggle to save consistently — the round-up mechanic saves money without requiring a decision each time.

## M-Shwari — savings + emergency loan
M-Shwari is a bank account-style product from Safaricom + NCBA Bank.

**Two features:**
1. **Lock savings**: set a target amount and lock-in period (1 month minimum). Earns 6.5% interest per year. You cannot withdraw before the lock period — this is the point.
2. **M-Shwari loan**: based on your saving and M-Pesa history. Loan amounts from KES 100–50,000. Interest: 7.5% for 30 days (much higher than a bank loan but lower than Fuliza for small amounts). Repayment in 30 days.

**Comparison with Fuliza:**
- Fuliza: instant, automatic, costs KES 2–20 per day continuously
- M-Shwari loan: applied for, costs 7.5% for the full 30 days
- For amounts > KES 1,000 held for more than 5 days, M-Shwari is usually cheaper than Fuliza

## The savings habit — why it works even at KES 20/day
The first goal is not accumulation — it is breaking the pattern of spending everything that arrives.

KES 20/day × 30 days = KES 600
KES 600 × 12 months = KES 7,200/year
KES 7,200 at 7% interest = KES 504 earned in interest

The interest is small. The real value is that you always have a buffer. You never start the month at KES 0.

## Which product to use
| Situation | Recommended product |
|---|---|
| Daily buffer, instant access needed | Ziidi |
| Saving toward a specific goal | Chumz or M-Shwari lock |
| Need emergency loan < 30 days | M-Shwari loan (if history built up) |
| Saving with classmates for a shared cost | Chumz group challenge |
| Replacing Fuliza habit | Ziidi buffer strategy |
